# Mobile Banking Reviews Analysis

This project was completed as part of graduate coursework in Business Analytics and AI at Northern Illinois University. It analyzes customer perceptions of banking services, with a focus on mobile app performance and its impact on customer satisfaction.

## Overview
- Analyzed multiple datasets of bank and mobile app reviews  
- Compared overall banking satisfaction vs. mobile app satisfaction  
- Applied natural language processing (NLP) techniques to extract insights from unstructured text data  
- Identified key drivers of negative sentiment in mobile experiences  
- Explored how app performance impacts customer ratings  

## NLP Techniques Used
- Tokenization using spaCy to process review text  
- Part-of-speech tagging to identify descriptive terms (e.g., adjectives)  
- Context-based analysis by extracting words surrounding key terms such as “app”  
- Frequency analysis of nouns and adjectives to identify common themes  
- Sentiment-oriented exploration of one-star and five-star reviews  

## Key Insights
- Customers generally rate overall banking services positively  
- Mobile app experiences show higher levels of negative sentiment  
- App performance and speed are major drivers of one-star reviews  
- Mobile-related issues are a leading cause of dissatisfaction  

## Tools Used
- Python (pandas, NumPy)  
- spaCy (NLP processing)  
- scikit-learn (supporting analysis)  
- matplotlib, seaborn (visualization)
